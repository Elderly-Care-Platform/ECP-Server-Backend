India is one of the best places in the world to age gracefully, as the young have always looked after the old.

Yet, ageing in India has its challenges:

  • The issues of ageing are not acknowledged enough; elder's needs are often ignored or misunderstood.
  • A lack of information and of professional support leaves families to deal with issues of ageing on their own.
  • Younger people find jobs in other places - in India and overseas. So more and more seniors find themselves living alone.

Older people are undergoing a change too; the new generation of "young seniors" don't want their kids to sacrifice their careers in order to take care of them; they are keen on being independent, and living with dignity. They would like their retirement years to be an age of active contribution.

Today, the definition of the term 'old' is different - we are not "old" even at 75!
